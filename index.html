Explain the difference between SN1 and SN2 more simply
2:15 PM
Think of it like this: SN2 is a one-step mugging — the nucleophile attacks at exactly the same moment the leaving group runs away. Everything happens together, so it's fast with primary substrates.

SN1 is a two-act play — first the leaving group leaves on its own, creating a carbocation. Then the nucleophile walks in and attacks from wherever it likes. This is why you get a racemic mixture.
