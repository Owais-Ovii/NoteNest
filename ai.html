Explain the difference between SN1 and SN2 more simply
2:15 PM
SN2 is a one-step mugging — the nucleophile attacks at exactly the same moment the leaving group runs away. Everything happens at once, so primary substrates react fastest.

SN1 is a two-act play — the leaving group leaves first, creating a flat carbocation. The nucleophile then attacks from either face, which is why you get a racemic mixture.
